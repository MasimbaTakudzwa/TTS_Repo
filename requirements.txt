Good call on questioning the pricing. Here's what I built and why I pivoted:

The free version uses Coqui XTTS v2 — it's open source, runs entirely on your machine, costs nothing, and the voice cloning quality is genuinely good. The only upfront cost is downloading the model (~1.8GB, happens once automatically).

To get started:

bash
pip install TTS pdfplumber
python main_free.py --text "Hello world" --ref my_voice.wav
The --ref flag is where the voice cloning happens — just point it at a WAV recording of any voice (6–30 seconds of clear speech) and it'll clone it. No recording? It falls back to a built-in voice automatically.

I also kept the ElevenLabs files in the folder in case you ever want to switch to it for production — the README has a full comparison table. A GPU will make a big difference on speed, but it'll work on CPU too.
